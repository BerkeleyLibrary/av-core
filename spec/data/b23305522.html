518    Originally broadcast on: KPFA, ca. 1993. 
520    Poet Opal Palmer Adisa interviews writer/poet Wanda 
       Coleman, author of Mad Dog, Black Lady, African Sleeping 
       Sickness and Hand Dance, among other books. Coleman 
       discusses when she found her poetic voice, talks about the
       function of poetry, her personal encounters with anti-
       Black discrimination, and about the reluctance of white 
       liberals to discuss issues that affect the Black 
       community. She also talks about the plight of the African 
       American community in South Central Los Angeles. The poems
       Coleman reads are A civilized plague, David Polion, Notes 
       of a cultural terrorist and Jazz wazz.
